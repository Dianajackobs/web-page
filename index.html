What We Do

We support with a range of gender based violence including: Domestic violence and abuse, financial abuse, tech abuse, emotional abuse, Rape and Sexual violence, Sex work and forced prostitution, Trafficking and Sexual exploitation, Stalking and harassment, Female genital mutilation, Forced marriage, FGM and so-called honour based violence and Child sexual exploitation. We offer free support, advice and information to those who have experienced abuse, by offering advice on rights, freedoms and services available to increase safety and reduce the risks posed. .

Who We Are

We are a team of safelives qualified independent domestic and sexual violence advocates also known as IDVA’s. We are domestic abuse experts who are passionate about supporting women escape abuse. We aim to be the link to a range of external agencies whose intervention is vital to guarantee the success of long term safety solutions for clients and families. Our role is to recognise the personal circumstance of each individual client and form a supportive, non judgmental informative relationship. Our priority is always the client’s safety and wellbeing. We will explore the options available to survivors and identify how best to achieve both immediate and long term safety. Empowering women with information on their rights and the recourses available to them in order to promote independence, assist them to become stronger and most importantly safe. So that they can begin to regain control over their lives, pursue justice and put the building blocks in place to begin a new life, free from fear.

Our Work

We have years of experience working with standard to high risk survivors for a short window from the point of crisis, we asses the risk level, discuss suitable options and develop safety plan. A critical aspect of our role is to develop a safety plan with the client in order to manage the risk of further harm violence or homicide by addressing the immediate threat. Safety plans usually address immediate safety and include practical steps that women can use to protect themselves and their children as well as longer term solutions. The impact of covid has seen a huge increase in the amount of survivors needing support. There is a lack of services available to client’s, and resources and services have been stretched beyond capacity leaving women and children left without vital support. We have vowed investigate all possible alternatives for my clients, to advocate strongly for them within the existing systems and even to look for more unconventional solutions to ensure that the client’s are able to reach safety. Our service is a free voluntary non profit run by and for survivors of abuse. We do this work because we care.
